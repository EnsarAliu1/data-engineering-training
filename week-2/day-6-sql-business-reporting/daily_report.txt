What I understood better today:
- I understood that revenue is not just about how many orders exist — it is about filtering only completed ones. If you include pending and cancelled orders in your revenue number, you are lying to yourself and to the business. I also understood that order count and revenue are two completely different metrics. Prishtina had the most orders but Vushtrri made more money. That kind of insight only appears when you actually read the results, not just run the query.

What was difficult:
- Understanding when to use HAVING versus WHERE was confusing at first. Both filter data, but they work at completely different stages of the query. WHERE runs before the rows are grouped, so it cannot see group totals. HAVING runs after grouping, so it can filter based on COUNT or SUM results. Once I understood the order of operations in SQL — FROM, JOIN, WHERE, GROUP BY, HAVING, SELECT, ORDER BY — it became much clearer.
