Daily Report - Day 14 Practice

Today I worked on:
- Designing and implementing a normalized 6-table relational database schema in SQLite for a tech training academy (students, instructors, programs, enrollments, attendance, and payments).
- Enforcing structural data integrity using Primary Keys, Foreign Keys, UNIQUE composite constraints, NOT NULL, and CHECK constraints.
- Writing comprehensive SQL query suites including multi-table JOINs, aggregation queries (GROUP BY), and group-filtered analytics (HAVING).
- Executing data quality audits using LEFT JOIN null-checks to spot process gaps, missing payment records, and ghost enrollments.
- Synthesizing technical database outputs into actionable business intelligence for executive decision-making.

Schema & Design tasks completed:
- Built a 3NF relational schema with bridge table architecture (`enrollments`) to resolve many-to-many relationships between students and programs.
- Configured referential integrity constraints across 1-to-N relationships (instructors to programs, enrollments to attendance, and enrollments to payments).
- Implemented robust `CHECK` rules restricting course levels (`Beginner`, `Intermediate`, `Advanced`), enrollment statuses (`active`, `completed`, `dropped`), payment statuses (`Pending`, `Paid`, `Refunded`), positive course prices, and non-negative session minutes.
- Added composite `UNIQUE` constraints to prevent duplicate course enrollments `(student_id, program_id)` and duplicate session attendance entries `(enrollment_id, session_date)`.

Testing & Quality Audit tasks completed:
- Wrote `relationship_tests.sql` to verify foreign key links, mandatory relationship constraints, and bridge table integrity.
- Executed `data_quality_checks.sql` to identify data anomalies, including missing payment records, unpaid active balances, and zero-attendance enrollments.
- Uncovered a business workflow anomaly (Enrollment ID 6 / Hana Morina) where an active course enrollment existed without a corresponding transaction record in the `payments` table.
- Identified financial exposure totaling €279.96 in uncollected fees across 4 active students with pending or missing payments.

Reporting & Business Analytics tasks completed:
- Generated course popularity and revenue reports comparing student volume against financial contribution (e.g., JavaScript Advanced vs HTML & CSS Fundamentals).
- Built geographic distribution reports highlighting student concentration (25% in Prishtinë, 50% combined in regional cities).
- Constructed `HAVING` clause reports filtering programs with revenue exceeding €300 and flagging students with attendance below 70%.
- Compiled executive recommendations in `business_insights.md` addressing payment gatekeeping, student retention alerts, and instructor workload distribution.

What I understood better today:
- How bridge tables decouple many-to-many relationships while providing a single anchor for secondary operational logs (attendance and payments).
- Why DDL foreign key constraints enforce relational integrity but cannot substitute for application transaction logic (e.g., ensuring payment creation upon enrollment).
- How `LEFT JOIN ... WHERE key IS NULL` serves as a powerful audit technique to catch system gaps and missing child records.

What was difficult:
- Constructing complex multi-level aggregations (`students` -> `enrollments` -> `attendance`) to correctly calculate percentage attendance rates for students with missing or zero session logs.

One result I can explain and defend:
- Why JavaScript Advanced (€299.98) generated double the collected revenue of HTML & CSS Fundamentals (€149.97) despite having fewer active students (2 vs 3). Higher course pricing in intermediate/advanced tiers yields superior margin per student compared to high-volume entry-level courses.

What I need to practice more:
- Designing automated triggers and database views to abstract complex reporting queries and enforce transactional rules directly within the database engine.
