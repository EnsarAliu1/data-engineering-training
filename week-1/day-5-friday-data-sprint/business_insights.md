---

**1. Total orders:**

We had 15 orders in total.

---

**2. Completed revenue:**

The total completed revenue is 3,950 EUR. We only counted orders that were marked as completed because those are the ones where the customer actually paid.

---

**3. City with most orders:**

Several cities had 2 orders each, so there was no single winner. Cities like Prishtina, Vushtrri, Peja, Ferizaj, Mitrovica, Gjilan, and Prizren all had 2 orders. Gjakova had only 1.

---

**4. Category with most orders:**

All three categories had the same number of orders. Electronics, Accessories, and Office each had 5 orders. Electronics made the most money though because the products cost more.

---

**5. Most valuable order:**

The most valuable order was from Blerim in Prishtina. He ordered 2 Smartphones at 450 EUR each, making a total of 900 EUR. That was the highest single order value in the whole dataset.

---

**6. Orders that should not count as revenue:**

Six orders should not be counted as real revenue. Three were still pending, one was still being processed, and two were cancelled. If an order is not completed, the business has not received the money yet, so it should not be counted.

---

**7. One business recommendation:**

The business should focus more on Electronics because those products bring in the most money per order. They should also make sure to follow up on pending orders so they do not lose sales that could still be completed.

---

**8. What we would show to the business owner:**

We would show the business owner the total confirmed revenue of 3,950 EUR and explain that there is another 1,285 EUR that was either lost or is still uncertain. We would also show which product category made the most money and point out that some orders were cancelled, which means lost sales that could be avoided.

---

**9. What Python helped us understand:**

Python helped us understand how the data works step by step. We wrote code that went through each order one by one, checked the status, and calculated the totals manually. This showed us exactly how the numbers are built and helped us understand the logic behind every result.

---

**10. What SQL helped us understand:**

SQL helped us get answers much faster and more simply. Instead of writing long code, we could ask the database a question in one or two lines and get the result instantly. It showed us that SQL is a very powerful tool for looking at data quickly, especially when you need to group or summarise information.
